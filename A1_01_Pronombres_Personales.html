Pronombres Personales

In this lesson you will learn all the subject pronouns in Spanish — yo, tú, él, ella and more — when to use them and when you can drop them completely.

📖 Explanation + chart 💬 Examples with translation ⚠️ Common mistakes ✏️ 6 practice exercises 🌍 Cultural note
💡 El concepto

Subject pronouns tell us who performs the action. In Spanish, they are often optional because the verb ending already shows who is speaking. You mainly use them for emphasis or clarity.

Los pronombres sujeto indican quién hace la acción. En español son opcionales — el verbo ya lo indica.

SINGULAR
yo I
tú informal you
usted formal you
él he
ella she
PLURAL
nosotros / nosotras we
vosotros / vosotras Spain you all
ustedes Lat. Am. you all
ellos they (m / mixed)
ellas they (f)
KEY POINT 1

Drop the pronoun when it's obvious. "Soy de Londres" is more natural than "Yo soy de Londres."

KEY POINT 2

tú vs. usted: Use tú with friends and family. Use usted with strangers, bosses, or in formal situations.

KEY POINT 3

él ≠ el. With an accent = he. Without = the. Context matters!

KEY POINT 4

nosotros/ellos = mixed or all-male groups. nosotras/ellas = all-female groups.

💬 Ejemplos con traducción
Yo soy de Nueva York, pero él es de Madrid.
I'm from New York, but he's from Madrid.
→ Both pronouns could be dropped — but here they add contrast.
¿Tú hablas español? — Sí, y ella también.
Do you speak Spanish? — Yes, and she does too.
→ Notice: no verb needed after "también" — "ella también" is enough.
Perdona, ¿usted trabaja en este hotel?
Excuse me, do you work at this hotel?
→ Use usted with people you don't know well, or in professional situations.
Nosotros llegamos mañana. Ellos llegan el martes.
We arrive tomorrow. They arrive on Tuesday.
→ The pronouns make it clear who is who — two different groups.
Ella trabaja en una cafetería y estudia por las noches.
She works in a café and studies in the evenings.
→ After the first "ella", the second verb (estudia) drops the pronoun — that's natural Spanish.
⚠️ Errores frecuentes
✗ Yo soy de Londres. Yo tengo 28 años. Yo trabajo en una oficina.
✓ Soy de Londres. Tengo 28 años. Trabajo en una oficina.
Overusing "yo" sounds unnatural. Drop it after the first mention — the verb ending makes it clear.
✗ Usted eres muy amable.
✓ Usted es muy amable.
"Usted" uses the 3rd person singular verb (es, tiene, trabaja…), not 2nd person (eres).
✗ Él y yo somos colegas. Nosotras trabajamos juntos.
✓ Nosotros trabajamos juntos.
A mixed-gender group (él + yo) uses nosotros, not nosotras.
✗ El trabaja en el aeropuerto.
✓ Él trabaja en el aeropuerto.
"él" (he) needs an accent mark. Without it, "el" = the (article).
🌍 Nota cultural

¿Vosotros o ustedes? It depends on where you are.

This is one of the biggest differences between Spanish from Spain and Spanish from Latin America:

  • Spain 🇪🇸 — uses vosotros/vosotras for informal "you all" with friends and family, and ustedes for formal situations.
  • Latin America 🌎 — vosotros is not used at all. Ustedes covers both formal and informal "you all."

💡 Good news: whichever variety you learn, you will be understood everywhere in the Spanish-speaking world. Both forms are correct — it's just a matter of where you are.
